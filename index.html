About

I'm Andrew Orkney, a postdoctoral researcher in the Hedrick Lab at Cornell University, College of Veterinary Medicine, Department of Biomedical Sciences.

Direction

I am a comparative evolutionary biologist with a broad training in the biological sciences. My principal love is animal skeletal morphology; I have published research in dinosaur histology, bird evolution, and I currently research ecomorphology, evolvability and anatomical organisation in diverse vertebrate groups, including bats. My multidisciplinary background also includes expertise in remote-sensing and marine ecology, and I have taken advantage of my capacity to bridge di fferent fields of research, gaining a diverse suite of skills. I am especially interested in the signi cance of biomechanical and developmental anatomical constraints that might be expected to restrict evolvability, and the solutions employed by real animal lineages to navigate these challenges. I am mentoring students to assemble new data sets, expanding by research to include sexually antagonistic selection on dimorphic traits across bats, and am collecting initial data to explore the intersection of canalisation and evolutionary versatility in squirrels and hummingbirds through the lens of assymetry. My range of research streams engages a diverse body of students in vibrant projects, with emphasis placed on developing foundational coding skills, learning phylogenetic comparative methods, and preparing students for research presentation at international conferences as part of the Hedrick lab.
